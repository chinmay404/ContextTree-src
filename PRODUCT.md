# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Explorers — people who think in branches: learners untangling hard topics,
coders comparing approaches, researchers running parallel inquiries,
writers drafting scenes multiple ways. They already use ChatGPT/Claude
daily and feel two pains: side questions pollute long chats, and
conversations are hostage to one provider. First paying segment:
AI-assisted long-form writers (they already stack $15–60/mo of tools).

## Product Purpose

Context Tree is a branching AI conversation studio on an infinite canvas.
Its core rule: every branch carries only its own lineage (scoped context).
Headline moves: continue any conversation on another AI, ask side
questions that never touch the main thread, compare branches and promote
the winner. BYOK ($59 one-time founding license). The landing page's one
job: make a curious visitor understand the three moves in under 30
seconds and click "Start exploring".

## Brand Personality

Precise, curious, honest. An instrument, not a toy — the feeling of a
well-made map or a night-sky chart. Confident enough to show the product
mechanism itself (trees, lineage, tokens) instead of abstract marketing
art. Speaks plainly; never hypes.

## Anti-references

- "ChatGPT but with branching" framing — banned (sherlocked Sept 2025).
- Generic SaaS landing: gradient hero text, big-number metrics, identical
  three-card feature grids, 3D shader backgrounds (the old landing had
  these; they were removed deliberately).
- Credit-reseller aesthetics (Flowith-style maximalism).
- Wordy sections — the owner's explicit feedback: explain visually,
  minimal copy, let diagrams and the tree icon carry meaning.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the mechanism.** The product's own artifacts (nodes, edges,
   lineage, model chips, token counts) ARE the visual language. If a
   section can be a diagram, it must be.
2. **Scoped context made felt.** Interactions demonstrate the core idea
   (hover a node → only its lineage lights) rather than claiming it.
3. **Two accents that mean something.** Amber = cross-AI continuation,
   teal = side question. Color encodes the product, never decorates.
4. **One line of copy per idea.** If text runs past a sentence, replace
   it with a picture.
5. **Honest numbers.** Costs, token counts, research citations — real
   figures, plainly labeled.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Keyboard-visible focus everywhere; prefers-reduced-motion honored (all
entrance/hover animation collapses to instant states); body text ≥4.5:1
contrast on the dark field; diagrams carry aria-labels describing what
they show.
